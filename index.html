Ritual

Taoist ritualsalso known as "fasting rituals" or "dojo", are religious activities in Taoism that communicate with the gods through specific rituals, pray for blessings and disasters, and save the souls of the dead. Its core idea is "celestial induction", that is, through rituals, spells, talismans, etc., to reconcile yin and yang, communicate with people and gods, and achieve the purpose of eliminating disasters and relieving suffering and relieving people.

Mainly divided into:
1. Praying for blessings: praying for the peace of the country and the people, good weather, family harmony, smooth career, etc.
2. Transcendence Dharma: Transcending the dead, resolving grievances and creditors, and appeasing lonely ghosts.
3. Disaster Relief Dharma: Exorcising evil, defusing disasters, and treating diseases.

Pilgrimage

Taoist cultivation of immortals originates from the pursuit of "longevity and long-term vision". Its thought is rooted in the philosophical system of "Taoism, Law, Nature" and "Unity of Heaven and Man", and advocates the transcendence and eternity of life through both internal and external cultivation.

The thought of immortal cultivation from a modern perspective:
1. Inspiration of physical and mental health: The breathing method and meditation in the practice of inner alchemy are used by modern people to reduce stress and improve concentration.
2. Echo of ecological philosophy: The concept of "Unity of Heaven and Man" in Taoism coincides with contemporary ecological protection thought.
3. Scientific controversy: The immortality of the body has not been scientifically confirmed, but practice is indeed beneficial to delaying aging and improving the quality of life.
